Drug Development

It is estimated that $2.6 billion is spent on drug development, but a large amount of that money is essentially wasted since 9 out of 10 drugs never make it to market.

Researchers developing new medicines are now able to use robots in order to analyze billions of data points, in order to predict and prove hypotheses. This saves both money and time.

“We are turning the drug-discovery paradigm upside down by using patient-driven biology and data to derive more-predictive hypotheses, rather than the traditional trial-and-error approach,”

– Niven Narain, Co-founder and Chief Executive of Berg, a biopharma company based in Boston
